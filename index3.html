About Us

BlessThee is a platform that makes several types of donations easier, convenient and more

effective. It comprises of sections for different types of donations which can be accessed

by NGOs and other groups dedicated to social work. This will help thousands of people to have a good meal,

proper education materials and also good pair of clothes to wear :)



We strive never to take the easy path, but always the honest one. We practice integrity in all our actions and try to do the right thing.

We are fiercely committed to our purpose of poverty alleviation, and work with a burning desire to make a difference.

We are committed to working together with unconditional respect, freedom, trust and support for each other.

Food donation

As the covid -19 crises continues to loom over us, BlessThee would provide much convenience to feed the poor. The leftover eatable food from licensed restaurants, shops, industrial and commercial kitchens will be collected on a daily basis through registrations. The occasional events such as weddings and big parties can also register for the donation. Even groceries can be donated.


“FOR HOMELESS, FOOD IS BIGGER WORRY THAN CORONA VIRUS”


Clothes donation

As we all have clothes that are not in use and are just occupying space which better be donated and can be a lot of help to someone in need of them. The users can input the amount of clothes to be donated and their address which will be collected into a database and ready for mass pick up by NGOs and partnering communities..


“IT IS NOT ABOUT HOW MUCH WE GIVE BUT HOW MUCH LOVE WE PUT INTO GIVING”


Stationery donation

Education is every child’s birth right. Unfortunately, there are too many kids who go to school but do not get basic stationary such as notebooks, pencils, erasers, pens, colour boxes, school bags etc. There are times when the kids do not even have text books even though they wish to study and appear for exams. Bags, stationeries and Books which are better to be distributed among children those who actually need them rather than selling at scrap.


“YOU HAVE TWO HANDS ONE TO HELP YOURSELF, SECOND TO HELP OTHERS”
